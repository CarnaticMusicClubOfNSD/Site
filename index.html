Our Journey

In 2022, a passionate high school student, Shreya Battula, envisioned a way to share her love for Carnatic music with the younger generation in her community. What began as a small after-school club at Canyon Creek Elementary with 5 students has grown to inspire 90+ students through a district-wide initiative. Now spanning 3 Northshore School District elementary schools—and still expanding—the program is led by 6 instructors and chapter heads, introducing young learners to the rich tradition of Indian classical music and proving how one student’s passion can create lasting community impact.


This journey wouldn't have been possible without the unwavering support of our Mentors Mr. Elliot Gray (Canyon Creek’s Music and Marimba teacher) and Mr. Srinivas Khedam (NSD's Assistant Director, Family Engagement). From the early days of planning to the club’s flourishing success, their guidance has been instrumental every step of the way.

Our Mission

The Carnatic Music Club is dedicated to making Indian classical music accessible to young students, fostering not only musical skills but also confidence, creativity, and cultural pride. Through structured lessons, mentorship, and performance opportunities, the club aims to:

  • Introduce children to the rich tradition of Carnatic music in a fun and engaging way.
  • Build a youth-led, sustainable model where student instructors gain leadership and teaching experience.
  • Encourage discipline, focus, and self-expression through consistent practice and learning.
  • Inspire a lasting appreciation for classical arts while creating a connected and supportive community of learners, educators, and families.

Curriculum & Structure

Carnatic music, a classical musical form originating in South India, is renowned internationally for its intricate melodies and rhythms. Developed over centuries and formalized around the 15th century, it is traditionally taught through a step-by-step system that cultivates both musical understanding and discipline.

At the Carnatic Music Club, students are introduced to this rich tradition starting with Sarali Swaras, the fundamental exercises that form the foundation of Carnatic music. The club follows an 11-week program designed with performance-based learning objectives, including:

  • Basic Carnatic music theory – understanding the structure and nuances of compositions
  • Vocal warmups and technique – building voice control and pitch accuracy
  • Core beginner-level sequences – developing skill and confidence
  • Live student performance showcase – celebrating progress and cultivating stage presence

This structured approach not only develops technical proficiency but also fosters creativity, confidence, and cultural appreciation, ensuring that each student experiences the joy of learning and performing a classical art form.
